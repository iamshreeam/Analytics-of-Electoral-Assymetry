# Analytics of Electoral Asymmetry 📊

**Public Policy and Opinion Cell | IIT Kanpur**  
*May 2025 – June 2025*

## 🎯 Objective
To analyze the **gender paradox in Indian democracy**, investigating the phenomenon where women outvote men but remain significantly underrepresented in political office.

## 🛠️ Approach
* **Electoral Data Analysis:** Analyzed data from **4 state elections** alongside the **2024 Lok Sabha election** to evaluate both electoral and non-electoral participation levels.
* **Global Benchmarking:** Benchmarked India's **15.5%** female representation against the **26–28% global average**, further partitioning the data by various demographics for deeper insights.

## 🚀 Impact
* Achieved a comprehensive data diagnosis, successfully identifying an **18–20 percentage point** non-electoral gender gap.
* Leveraged these findings to propose **6+ targeted policy reforms** aimed at addressing the asymmetry.

## 🔎 Focus Areas
* Data Analytics & Visualization
* Public Policy & Democratic Representation
* Gender Demographics
* Electoral & Non-Electoral Participation
